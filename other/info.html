Albert Einstein

Background


The parents of Albert Einstein were middle-class, secular Jews. Hermann Einstein, his father, began his career as a featherbed salesman and then successfully ran an electrochemical plant. The family home was managed by his mother, Pauline Koch. Maria, his only sibling, was born two years after Albert and went by the name Maja. Albert Einstein was a German-born physicist who later lived in the U.S. is regarded as one of the most influential physicists of all time. He studied everything from motion and gravity theories to quantum mechanics. Following the publication of a number of ground-breaking studies, Einstein traveled the globe and delivered presentations about his findings. In 1903 Einstein married Milena Maric, a Serbian physics student whom he had met at school in Zurich. They had three children: a daughter, named Lieserl, and two sons, named Hans and Eduard. After a period of unrest, Einstein and Maric divorced in 1919. Einstein, during his marriage, had begun an affair with his cousin Elsa Lowenthal. They were married in 1919, the same year he divorced Maric.

Scientific Inventions

While employed at a Swiss patent office during his "Miracle Year" in 1905, Einstein published four significant papers:

  • Max Planck, a German physicist, developed the quantum theory, which Einstein used to explain the photoelectric phenomenon in his first publication. For this work, he went on to receive the Nobel Prize in 1921.
  • The first experimental evidence for the existence of atoms was shared in his second publication using the Brownian motion, or the motion of microscopic particles suspended in liquid, according to Britannica.
  • In his third paper, "On the Electrodynamics of Moving Bodies," Einstein's special theory of relativity was presented.
  • His fourth paper led to the most famous equation in science, E = mc2. According to this equation, mass and energy are essentially the same physical thing. Despite the fact that Einstein was never requested to join the American-led Manhattan Project, which developed the atomic bomb, this equation and other theories of his contributed to its development.
  • Unified field theory, which uses a single theoretical framework to describe fundamental forces and the connections between elementary particles, was Einstein's final project, which was unfinished when he passed away.
