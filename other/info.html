Albert Einstein

Background


The parents of Albert Einstein were middle-class, secular Jews. Hermann Einstein, his father, began his career as a featherbed salesman and then successfully ran an electrochemical plant. The family home was managed by his mother, Pauline Koch. Maria, his only sibling, was born two years after Albert and went by the name Maja. Albert Einstein was a German-born physicist who later lived in the U.S. is regarded as one of the most influential physicists of all time. He studied everything from motion and gravity theories to quantum mechanics. Following the publication of a number of ground-breaking studies, Einstein traveled the globe and delivered presentations about his findings. In 1903 Einstein married Milena Maric, a Serbian physics student whom he had met at school in Zürich. They had three children: a daughter, named Lieserl, and two sons, named Hans and Eduard. After a period of unrest, Einstein and Maric divorced in 1919. Einstein, during his marriage, had begun an affair with his cousin Elsa Löwenthal. They were married in 1919, the same year he divorced Maric.
